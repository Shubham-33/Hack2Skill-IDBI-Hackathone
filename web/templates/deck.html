How it works

3-layer architecture · numbers never hallucinated

Directives (SOPs)→ AI Orchestration (LLM)→ Deterministic Engine + ML

Engine owns every number

Eligibility, rate, EMI, commission, clawback, health score — auditable & reproducible.

ML owns prediction

Conversion propensity & partner default risk. The PD feeds risk-based pricing — higher-risk leads auto-loaded within the approved band.

LLM only phrases

Recommendations, pitches, PDF copy, chat. De-identified input; compliance-checked output.

{{ (d.risk_auc*100)|round|int }}%

AUC · Gini {{ d.risk_gini }} · adds the sourcing partner's track record to catch adverse selection before disbursal. Bayesian shrinkage keeps it fair to small DSAs.

Reported as Gini (= 2·AUC−1), the metric bank model-risk teams cite. Every score ships per-decision reason codes (occlusion attribution) — and a declined lead gets Reg-B / RBI adverse-action reason codes. No black box. Figures are on synthetic data (indicative); in production the models retrain on IDBI's real conversion + NPA history. Partner history is a real but secondary signal — no target leakage. The queue ranks by risk-adjusted value = P(convert) × (net interest income − expected credit loss), so a loan that would convert but destroy value is correctly down-ranked.
